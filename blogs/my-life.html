My Life

May 8, 2025

Life is a journey filled with unexpected twists and turns. My own path has been no exception. Growing up with a passion for technology, I was always the kid taking apart gadgets to see how they worked. Sometimes I'd even manage to put them back together correctly!

My academic journey culminated at ABC University, where I earned my B.S. in Computer Engineering. Those years were transformative, challenging me intellectually and introducing me to lifelong friends and mentors. My capstone project on FPGA-accelerated inference sparked my interest in the intersection of hardware and AI, a passion that continues to drive my career today.

After graduation, I took the entrepreneurial plunge, co-founding an autonomous-drone startup. Those two years were intense - designing control stacks, working on onboard vision systems, and pitching to investors. We secured $500k in seed funding, a milestone that validated our vision but also taught me about the challenges of bringing complex hardware to market.

Today, as a Research Scientist at XYZ Lab, I'm fortunate to work at the cutting edge of multimodal LLM alignment. Every day brings new challenges and insights, pushing the boundaries of what's possible with AI. Publishing my first peer-reviewed paper was a career highlight that made those late nights and debugging sessions all worthwhile.

Outside of work, I find balance through hiking, amateur photography, and teaching coding to middle school students. These activities remind me why I fell in love with technology in the first place - its power to create, connect, and transform our world for the better.

As I look to the future, I'm excited about the possibilities that lie ahead in AI research and its applications. The field is evolving rapidly, and I'm grateful to be part of this moment in technological history. Whatever comes next, I'm ready for the adventure.
